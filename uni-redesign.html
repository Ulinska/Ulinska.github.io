University website redesign

Project overview

Let’s talk about this project

This project was done for my academic class. The task was to redesign the main page of our university department -WDiB (this design functioned up untill 2022). It was split into 4 main points -

  • learning about our users
  • research of basic structure of academic websites
  • creating appropriate information architecture and wireframes
  • and finally - creating the final design

Research and card sorting was done in groups, but design was up to each student.

Problem

Current design is confusing not only for candidates, but also for students and lecturers.

Goal

Redesign and organize information to make it more approachable for candidates and less confusing for students and lecturers.

Understand

Academic sites are quite hard to handle as they are aimed towards various types of users with different needs. This means a lot of information have to be displayed right of the bat on index page. It also means navigation will become content heavy as well, as it needs to lead to all corners of the page.
Even thou students and lecturers are considered to be on the "smart side" it does not mean the website can be confusing or hard to use. People are not always computer savvy, have various disabilities or just want to use a website quickly without learning the ropes are going to use it.

User types and personas

Our user groups are: students, lecturers and staff, college candidates.
Should we prioritize any of those groups?
If we look at the site from the perspective of business, the most valuable users will be future students. Page should showcase the strong point of the university and reasons why should they chose this institution over the other.
We will treat this group as main priority while designing the main page, as it's theirs first and most important contact with the institution. Students and staff will be included in study and provided with all necessary features.

Understand

Competitive analyze

We have checked about 15 websites from all over the world to learn and understand what are crucial elements found on academic websites. Let’s analyze it from heads to toes!

Navigation

First of Navigation. It’s a crucial part to have well organized navigation suited to everyone’s needs.
To make it easier, many pages can be moved to an extra navigation under the hero section.It's great way to highlight the most urgent and interesting topics. This also takes some weight from the main navigation

Hero section

Hero section should be aimed towards people that come to this page for the first time - college candidates. It has to highlight either elements that the university takes pride in the most or the general vibe of the place.

News

Next the news! For the convenience of students and lecturers, news should always be displayed on the main page. To add even more convenience it can be also split in two times, general news and events. This way important events won’t get lost in a ton of information about holidays and deadlines.

Social proof

The social proof. This part of the page makes it more creditable and more real. There are a few types of proof.
First are works of students and lecturers. It’s the best way to attract new student by showing them what they can achieve with our help.
Next are statistics. Number make it easier to understand how grand (or cozy) the place is.
The last are real people! Showing who works or studies here is a great way to make the place feel real, even on the screen.

Studies

Main majors and programs are also important. New students need guidance and clear explanation what degree are they looking at. Making them feel welcomed is the first step to gain a new student.
